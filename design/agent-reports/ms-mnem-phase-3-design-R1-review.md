## Critical

- **[C1-RESIDUAL] The per-card fold fixed only the two CO-LOCATED decode-and-derive loops; it does NOT address the DECODE-HERE / STORE / DERIVE-AND-EMIT-LATER path in `bundle.rs`, which has no mechanism to carry per-card language. The fix as written is mechanically unrealizable for the `--import-json` multisig path.**
  - Ground truth: `ResolvedSlot` (= `CosignerKeyInfo`, `synthesize.rs:219`) is defined at `synthesize.rs:616-637` with fields `xpub`, `fingerprint`, `path`, `entropy: Option<Zeroizing<Vec<u8>>>`, `master_xpub`, `_entropy_pin`. **There is NO per-slot language field.**
  - The `--import-json` ms1 path decodes each `envelope.bundle.ms1[i]` at `bundle.rs:1613`, extracts entropy through the `Payload::Entr` arm (`:1618-1625`, wildcard `_ => Err` at `:1620`), then at `bundle.rs:1626` does `resolved_slots[i].entropy = Some(entropy_bytes)` and **discards the wire language**. Derivation and ms1 re-emit happen LATER: derive at `bundle.rs:626` via `derive_slot::derive_bip32_from_entropy(…, lang, …)` and per-slot ms1 EMIT at `synthesize.rs:291` and `synthesize.rs:778` (`match &c.entropy / &s.entropy … Payload::Entr`). At those later sites the per-card language is GONE because it was never stored on the slot.
  - Consequence — the exact C1 bug, unfixed at this site: an `--import-json` bundle whose `ms1[i]` is a Japanese `mnem` card will (a) re-derive cosigner i's xpub under the run's `--language`/English at `bundle.rs:626` → wrong xpub / wrong parent-fp cross-check, and (b) re-emit cosigner i as an `Entr` card at `synthesize.rs:291/778` (language LOST). Both silent.
  - Why the fold missed it: the C1 fix pattern (`payload_bip39_language(payload, cli)` called *inside the loop from the decoded payload in hand*) only works where decode and derive are co-located — true at `verify_bundle.rs:2400/2411` and `overlay.rs:127/161` (both fixed correctly), but FALSE for `bundle.rs:1613→1626` (decode) → `bundle.rs:626` (derive) → `synthesize.rs:291/778` (emit), three different functions separated by the `ResolvedSlot` boundary.
  - **Fix:** add a per-slot language field to `ResolvedSlot` (e.g. `pub language: Option<bip39::Language>` — `Some(wire_code_to_bip39(code))` for a decoded `Mnem` card, `None`/English for `Entr` and non-ms1 sources). Populate at the import-json decode (`bundle.rs:1618` Mnem arm) and at every slot-resolution arm. Read it at the derive site (`bundle.rs:619/626` — pass the per-slot bip39 language) and at the emit sites (`synthesize.rs:291/778` — choose `Entr` vs `Mnem{code}` per slot from that field). Step 4 and Step 5 must NAME `ResolvedSlot`/`CosignerKeyInfo`, the new field, and these three sites; the current "thread the per-card language to wherever that slot's entropy is later derived" (Step 4 bundle bullet) and "for bundle, per slot" (Step 5) are hand-waves that do not survive contact with the struct, which has nowhere to put the language. Add a test: `--import-json` envelope with a ja-mnem `ms1[0]` + an en-entr `ms1[1]` → `bundle` re-derives correctly per cosigner AND re-emits `ms1[0]` as mnem-ja, `ms1[1]` as entr.

## Important

- **[I4-RESIDUAL-a] `convert.rs:1468` (the `--from ms1 --to phrase` DERIVE) is an ms1-reachable derive site that Step 4 gestures at but does not name; it will mis-derive a non-English mnem source.** In the `Ms1` source arm (`convert.rs:1445-1481`), `:1446` decodes, `:1452-1459` extracts entropy (the `_ => Err` wildcard at `:1454`), but `:1468` does `Mnemonic::from_entropy_in(language.into(), &entropy[..])` where `language = args.language.unwrap_or_default()` (`convert.rs:1146`). A `convert --from ms1 --to phrase` on a ja-mnem card re-renders the phrase under English → wrong phrase, silently. Name `convert.rs:1468` explicitly and route it through `payload_bip39_language(&payload, args.language())` (decode and derive ARE co-located in the same arm — the easy in-arm fix, just under-cited).

## Minor

- **[M5] C2's load-bearing test is sufficient as specified, with one tightening.** The proposed identity test (a) `wire_code_to_bip39(c) == bip39::Language::from(wire_code_to_cli(c).unwrap())` catches a wrong code→language mapping ONLY IF `wire_code_to_cli` and `wire_code_to_bip39` are independent tables (a single shared lookup makes it vacuously true). Since the plan mandates THREE separate explicit matches, the identity is non-vacuous. To catch a mapping wrong in BOTH tables consistently, the label-pin (c) `MNEM_LANGUAGE_NAMES[c] == <expected literal>` is the backstop — pin all 10 labels (not a spot-check), and add anchor assertions `wire_code_to_bip39(1) == bip39::Language::Japanese` + `wire_code_to_bip39(4) == bip39::Language::SimplifiedChinese` so the Chinese-swap case is pinned to external bip39 truth, not just internal self-consistency.

- **[M6] Step 6 advisory re-key has a per-slot vs per-bundle granularity mismatch (couples to C1-RESIDUAL).** `bundle.rs:737-742` fires ONE advisory off `args.language.unwrap_or_default()` at the bundle level, but the emit decision is per-slot. Once the per-slot language field exists, re-state Step 6 as "suppress the bundle advisory iff EVERY secret-bearing slot emits a self-describing mnem card; otherwise fire." The `convert.rs:1009/1017` single-source re-key is clean (one source ⇒ `args.language` == effective).
